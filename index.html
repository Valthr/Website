The Challenge

Refinery logistics, rethought from the air.

BAPCO's 2.5 × 2 km refinery still moves lab samples, spare parts and safety gear by hand. Every errand means delays and sends people through hazardous zones that drones could cross in minutes.

Manual transport ties up skilled technicians in routine logistics, slows lab turnaround, and leaves critical work waiting on a courier who's still walking across the site.

The Valthr Proposal

A fleet of twenty drones, one intelligent dispatcher.

Valthr deploys 20 electric drones across distributed micro-bases on the refinery footprint, coordinated by a central operations hub. An AI-driven dispatcher allocates every task in real time based on priority, payload, battery state and proximity, so the right drone is always already en route.

The system is built for hazardous industrial environments. Intrinsically safe components, geofenced corridors, redundant communications and automated fail-safe protocols keep the fleet predictable even when the site isn't.

What Drones Carry

Two payload classes, one network.

01 / Lab Samples

On-demand sample transport

Time-critical oil, water and product samples flown straight from the process units to the central lab, cutting turnaround from hours to minutes.

02 / Tools, Spares & Safety Kit

Small parts, delivered where they're needed

Spare parts, specialist tools and emergency safety equipment routed on demand to any work site, so crews stop waiting and keep working.
